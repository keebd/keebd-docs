# Build Steps

The Reviung 5 is a Pro Micro board, so unlike a fully through-hole kit there are
only a handful of components to solder. We'll prepare the controller first, then
work through the PCB from the lowest components upward before assembling the
plates.

![Front of the Reviung 5 PCB](./pcb-front.jpg?height=500px)

:::tip
The LEDs (Step 3) and rotary encoder (Step 6) are optional. If you're not
fitting them, simply skip those steps.
:::

## 1. Prepare the Pro Micro

![Pro Micro with Conthrough spring-pin headers soldered on](./promicro-headers.jpg?height=500px)

The Pro Micro is socketed onto the PCB using two Conthrough spring-pin headers,
so it can be removed later if needed. Solder the two headers to the Pro Micro
with the end whose holes sit **closer to the pins** inserted into the Pro Micro.

Before soldering, flash your firmware while the board is still easy to handle:

1. Install [QMK Toolbox](https://github.com/qmk/qmk_toolbox/releases).
2. Connect the Pro Micro over USB and flash the Reviung 5 firmware (a VIA/REMAP
   compatible build is available, so you can remap keys later in
   [REMAP](https://remap-keys.app/)).

:::info
The Reviung 5 uses a Micro-USB connector on the Pro Micro. Reinforcing the
connector with a little glue is a good idea, as Micro-USB ports are prone to
tearing off with repeated use.
:::

## 2. Solder the Reset Switch

![Tactile reset switch soldered to the back of the PCB](./reset-switch.jpg?height=500px)

Solder the tactile reset switch to the **back** of the PCB, in the position
marked `RESET`. This lets you put the board into bootloader mode for future
firmware flashing.

## 3. Install the LEDs (Optional)

![WS2812B LEDs soldered to the PCB](./led-soldered.jpg?height=500px)

If you're fitting the optional WS2812B RGB LEDs, install them now. There are up
to four positions (`RGB1`–`RGB4`); work through them in order and **test each
LED before moving on to the next** — a single bad joint will stop every LED
after it in the chain from lighting.

![Diagram showing the correct WS2812B LED orientation](./led-orientation.jpg?height=400px)

:::info
LEDs are orientation-sensitive. Line up the notched (cut) corner of the LED with
the `GND ▲` marking on the PCB.
:::

Use a temperature-controlled iron at around 320°C. Apply flux, pre-tin one pad,
seat the LED with tweezers, tack it down on the pre-tinned pad, then solder the
remaining three pads. Clean off the flux and test before continuing.

## 4. Install the Diodes

![1N4148 diodes soldered to the PCB](./diodes.jpg?height=500px)

Install the 5 x 1N4148 diodes, one near each switch position.

:::info
Diodes are orientation-sensitive. The black bar (the `|` mark) on the diode must
line up with the `|` side of the `▷|` marking on the PCB.
:::

Bend the legs, insert each diode from the front, solder from the back, then clip
the excess legs flush. Re-flow each cut joint briefly so the exposed metal is
sealed with solder.

## 5. Test the Board

![Testing key input by bridging the switch pads with tweezers](./function-test.jpg?height=500px)

Before assembling the case, connect the board to your PC and confirm every key
registers. Bridge each switch's two solder pads with tweezers (or a snipped
diode leg) and check that a keypress is sent. Fixing a cold joint now is far
easier than after the switches are on.

## 6. Install the Rotary Encoder (Optional)

If you're fitting the optional rotary encoder, solder it to the PCB now, before
the top plate goes on. If your encoder uses the extra SMD diode position, fit
that `1N4148W` diode as well.

## 7. Attach the Pro Micro Protection Cover

![Pro Micro protection cover fitted to the top plate](./promicro-cover.jpg?height=500px)

Attach the acrylic Pro Micro protection cover to the top plate using the short
M2 screws, spacers and nuts.

:::caution
Do not overtighten the screws — acrylic parts can crack. Snug is enough.
:::

## 8. Fit the Switches to the Top Plate

![MX switches fitted into the top plate](./switches-plate.jpg?height=500px)

Push your MX switches into the top plate until they click home. Make sure each
switch is fully seated and that its pins are straight and not bent under the
housing.

## 9. Mount the Top Plate onto the PCB

![Top plate with switches lowered onto the main PCB](./mount-plate-pcb.jpg?height=500px)

Lower the top plate (with switches fitted) onto the main PCB, guiding all of the
switch pins through their holes. Check from the side that every switch is seated
flush before you solder anything.

## 10. Solder the Switches

![Switches being soldered from the back of the PCB](./solder-switches.jpg?height=500px)

With the switches fully seated, solder each pin from the back of the PCB. Clean
off any flux residue when you're done.

## 11. Attach the Bottom Plate & Feet

![Bottom plate and rubber feet attached](./bottom-plate.jpg?height=500px)

Fit the metal spacers to the bottom plate with M2 screws, place the top
plate + PCB assembly on top, and secure it by screwing down from the top-plate
side. Finally, stick a rubber foot in each of the four corners of the bottom
plate.

## 12. Install the Keycaps

![Keycaps installed on the Reviung 5](./keycaps.jpg?height=500px)

Press on your keycaps and you're all done!
